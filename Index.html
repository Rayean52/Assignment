Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a globally acclaimed social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate from Bangladesh. He is best known as the founder of the Grameen Bank and the pioneer of microcredit and microfinance, revolutionary concepts that provide small, collateral-free loans to impoverished individuals, especially women, to empower them economically. Through his efforts, Dr. Yunus has significantly contributed to alleviating poverty and fostering entrepreneurship in marginalized communities worldwide.
