Bangladesh's first satellite, Bangabandhu-1, marked a significant milestone in the country's technological advancement and its entry into the global space industry. Launched on May 11, 2018, from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida, USA, aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket, the satellite was named after the Father of the Nation, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman.Operated by the Bangladesh Communication Satellite Company Limited (BCSCL), Bangabandhu-1 is a geostationary communication satellite positioned at 119.1° East longitude.

The value of the Bangladeshi Taka (BDT) in relation to the US Dollar (USD) plays a crucial role in Bangladesh's economy, influencing trade, remittances, and foreign investment. As a developing nation with a strong reliance on exports, particularly garments, and remittance inflows from expatriate workers, the exchange rate directly impacts economic stability. Over time, the Taka has faced depreciation against the US Dollar due to factors such as inflation, trade deficits, and global economic pressures.
